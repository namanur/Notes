**Naman:** I would prefer mid-college students. If they have a 4-year degree, I'd love if they are at 2.5 years or something. ## What Was Concluded

**The Core Problem Being Solved:**
Context that doesn't accumulate and knowledge that doesn't transfer — for students whose entry-level jobs are being automated faster than universities or human mentors can track.

**The Real Product:**
A personalized career navigation system for 2nd/3rd year college students in engineering, finance, and other automating-fast fields. It interviews them, ingests their notes and interests, maps their current skills against what the market wants *right now*, and builds a custom curriculum with real industry context. Like a mentor who is simultaneously inside the industry AND reading the live job market.

**The Unfair Advantage:**
Even a 4-year industry veteran can't give accurate advice right now because the market is changing faster than human experience can track. Only a system reading live job postings, industry shifts, and the student's specific profile simultaneously can do this accurately.

**The Business Model:**
Free tier (their compute, your system) → prove it works → paid tier for power users → student proof becomes the case study that sells to companies.

**The Distribution:**
LinkedIn + Twitter, building in public. Students become users and validators simultaneously.

**The Real Year 1 Goal:**
Not revenue. Not scale. **Proof of capability.** One working product, real students using it, documented results — enough to enter the circle of people building serious things and see what Peak B looks like from there.

**The Strategic Decision Made:**
The product waits. The foundation doesn't. Linux, Python, agents, LLM wiki, understanding limitations — all of it first. Exit criterion: can you build and deploy the student platform yourself without hitting a wall you can't debug.

**The Known Risk:**
Planning-execution oscillation. The pattern of deep analysis substituting for action. Named, not yet solved. To be addressed in the execution map.
