# Personal Knowledge Management (PKM)

Personal Knowledge Management is the practice of capturing, organizing, and retrieving information to turn it into personal knowledge. In this vault, PKM is the "infrastructure" that supports all future learning phases.

## 1. Markdown: The Language of the Vault
Markdown is a lightweight markup language with plain-text-formatting syntax. It ensures that notes are future-proof, human-readable, and easily parsed by tools like Obsidian and [[Git and GitHub Setup|GitHub]].

### Core Syntax
- **Headers**: Use `#` for H1, `##` for H2, etc.
- **Lists**: Use `-` or `*` for bullets, and `1.` for numbered lists.
- **Code**: Use backticks for `inline code` and triple backticks for blocks.
- **Emphasis**: Use `*italic*` and `**bold**`.
- **Links**: `[Link Text](URL)` or `Internal Link`.

## 2. Note Taking: Atomic and Evolutionary
Effective note-taking in a technical PKM system follows two main principles:
- **Atomicity**: Each note should focus on a single concept or idea. This makes them easier to link and reuse.
- **Evolution**: Notes are not static. They start as "fleeting notes" (daily logs), move to "literature notes" (summaries of research), and finally become "permanent notes" (the Knowledge layer).

## 3. Linking Ideas: The Second Brain
The power of Obsidian lies in **Internal Links** `Note Name`. 
- **Bidirectional Linking**: When you link Note A to Note B, Obsidian tracks the connection both ways.
- **Emergent Structure**: Instead of a rigid folder hierarchy, structure emerges from how notes are connected.
- **Contextual Knowledge**: Linking allows you to see how "Computer Architecture" (Phase 2) relates to "Operating Systems" (Phase 3) without duplicating information.

## 4. The PKM Mindset
- **Externalize Thinking**: Don't keep complex architectures in your head; move them to Markdown.
- **Reduce Friction**: Keep the system simple so you actually use it.
- **Precision of Thought**: Writing forces you to define terms and logic clearly, which is essential for engineering.
